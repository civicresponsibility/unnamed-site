About

We are empowering people to shape the U.S. Congress through targetted, small-dollar donations to candidates. Until we reach the future where money plays no role in politics, let’s at least give small-dollar donors a fighting chance.

How it works

When there is an upcoming vote in Congress you have an opinion about, come to if.then.fund to pledge a donation to members of Congress who vote your way or to their opponents in the next election if they vote against you.

After the vote, we’ll split your contribution as many ways as needed — as low as 1¢ per recipient — and we’ll tell those members of Congress why you made your contribution.

Why it works

Measures real outcomes
Campaign contributions made here on if.then.fund are based on what legislators do, not what they say they will do.
Shapes the next Congress
Keep ’em in or throw ’em out. Donations can be made to incumbents who support your position or to their general election challenger if they vote the other way.
Aggregates small-dollar donations
When aggregated together, many small-dollar donations (even down to 1¢) can add up to something candidates will pay more attention to.
Reaches beyond a letter
Members of Congress may only be accountable to their constituents, but committee chairs and party leaders make policy for us all. if.then.fund lets you shape the whole Congress.

Some questions you may have

Is this bribery?
No. Bribery laws require that the giver intends to influence an official act. Because we do not reveal any information before the vote that could influence its outcome, there is no influence of that act.

Team

Jonathan Zucker, co-founder

Jonathan Zucker is a political technology entrepreneur. He is the founder & CEO of Democracy Engine, which helps organizations channel funds to candidates and causes to effect real change. Prior to Democracy Engine, he was executive director of ActBlue.

He is also an advisor and counsel to CrowdPAC and co-founder and COO of Ruck.us. He holds a J.D. from Georgetown University. (extended bio)

Joshua Tauberer, co-founder

Joshua Tauberer is a civic hacker and entrepreneur. His GovTrack.us project, which launched in 2004, helps millions of individuals track and understand the activities of the U.S. Congress, and it was the first website to create open data for Congress.

He is also the author of Open Government Data: The Book, an organizer of Open Data Day DC, and holds a Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania. (homepage)

About

We are empowering Americans to shape the U.S. Congress through targeted, small-dollar donations to candidates.

How it works

Is there an upcoming vote in Congress you have an opinion about? Schedule a donation to members of Congress who vote the way you want or to the next election challenger of members of Congress who vote against you.

After the vote, we’ll split your contribution across those members of Congress and challengers — even down to 1¢ per candidate if needed — and we’ll tell the politicians why you made your contribution.

Is this legal? Yes. Bribery laws require that the giver intends to influence an official act. Because we do not reveal any information about your planned contribution before the vote, there is no possibility that you (or us) are influencing an official act.

Why we’re doing this

By targeting real outcomes, shaping the next Congress, aggregating donations, and reaching all members of Congress, we hope to raise the tide of small-donor supporters and shift the balance of power away from concentrated wealth. Read our theory of change for more details.

Team

Jonathan Zucker is a political technology entrepreneur. He is the founder & CEO of Democracy Engine, which helps organizations channel funds to candidates and causes to effect real change. Prior to Democracy Engine, he was executive director of ActBlue.

He is also an advisor and counsel to CrowdPAC and co-founder and COO of Ruck.us. He holds a J.D. from Georgetown University. extended bio

Joshua Tauberer, co-founder

Joshua Tauberer is a civic hacker and entrepreneur. His GovTrack.us project, which launched in 2004, helps millions of individuals track and understand the activities of the U.S. Congress, and it was the first website to create open data for Congress.

He is also the author of Open Government Data: The Book, an organizer of Open Data Day DC, and holds a Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania. homepage

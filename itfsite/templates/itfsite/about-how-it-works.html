How It Works

The basics

When there is an upcoming vote in Congress you have an opinion about, use if.then.fund to schedule a donation to members of Congress based on the outcome of the vote.

After the vote, we’ll split your donation across the representatives and senators in Congress depending on how they vote. If they vote the way you wanted, they’ll get a portion of your donation. If they vote the other way, their next opponent in a general election will get their portion of your donation instead. We can split your donation all the way down to 1¢ per recipient if needed!

What are the fees?

if.then.fund applies a 5% fee to donations. Our partner Democracy Engine applies a 4% + 20¢ credit card processing fee to your total donation. Fees are deducted from your donation amount.

How does a donation to the next general election opponent work?

Donations made to throw out an elected official are a donation to the general election opponent of that elected official in their next election. The opponent will be the candidate of the other major party. The oppponent may not be known at the time you make your contribution. Our partner Democracy Engine will hold your contribution until the opponent is known following the next primary election. By law Democracy Engine must distribute your funds within 10 days of the opponent becoming known.

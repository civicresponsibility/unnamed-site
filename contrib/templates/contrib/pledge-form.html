Contributor requirements

Federal law restricts who can make a campaign contribution to a candidate for office:

You must be at least eighteen years old and be either a U.S. Citizen or lawfully admitted permanent resident (i.e., a green card holder). You cannot be employed directly by the federal government as a contractor. You must make this contribution with your own funds, which were not provided to you by any other person or entity. You must use your own personal credit card, and not a corporate or business card or a card issued to another person.

If you make more than $200 in contributions to any one candidate in a calendar year, your name, address, employment, and contribution amount become a part of the public record.
